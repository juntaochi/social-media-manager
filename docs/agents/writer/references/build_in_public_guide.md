# Build in Public Style Guide

## What is Build in Public?

Build in Public (BiP) is a content strategy where creators and entrepreneurs share their journey of building products, companies, or projects openly and transparently. Instead of only announcing finished products, you share the process: the wins, the struggles, the learnings, and the failures.

### Core Principles

1. **Transparency**: Share real numbers, real challenges, real outcomes
2. **Authenticity**: Be yourself, don't put on a corporate mask
3. **Consistency**: Regular updates, not just major milestones
4. **Value-First**: Help others learn from your experience
5. **Community**: Engage with your audience, don't just broadcast

## Why Build in Public?

**For You:**
- Builds an audience before launch
- Gets early feedback and validation
- Creates accountability
- Establishes authority and trust
- Generates free marketing through storytelling

**For Your Audience:**
- Educational content they can learn from
- Authentic connection with the creator
- Behind-the-scenes access
- Opportunity to influence the product
- Entertainment value of following a journey

## Content Structure

### The Hook (First 1-2 Lines)
**Purpose**: Make people stop scrolling

**Effective hooks:**
- Surprising numbers: "Reduced our AWS bill by 80%"
- Confessions: "I almost quit building this..."
- Questions: "Ever wondered why X?"
- Announcements: "Just shipped the feature I've been dreaming about"
- Lessons: "Here's what 6 months of building taught me"

**Weak hooks:**
- "Update time" (boring, vague)
- "Here's a thread" (obvious, adds no value)
- "Been working hard" (everybody works hard)

### The Body (Middle Tweets/Sections)
**Purpose**: Deliver value and substance

**Structure options:**

**Problem â†’ Solution:**
```
1. Here was the challenge...
2. We tried X, didn't work
3. Then we tried Y, better
4. Final solution: Z
```

**Before â†’ After:**
```
1. Where we started...
2. What we changed...
3. Where we are now...
4. What we learned...
```

**Step-by-Step:**
```
1. First we did X...
2. Then Y...
3. Finally Z...
4. Results: ...
```

**Insight â†’ Evidence:**
```
1. Main lesson learned
2. Why it matters
3. Data/example supporting it
4. How to apply it
```

### The Close (Final Tweet/Section)
**Purpose**: Drive engagement and action

**Good closes:**
- **Question**: "What's your approach to this?"
- **CTA**: "Try it out: [link]"
- **Acknowledgment**: "Thanks to everyone who helped!"
- **Invitation**: "Would love your feedback on..."
- **Tease**: "More on this next week..."

**Weak closes:**
- Ending abruptly with no engagement hook
- "That's all for now" (adds nothing)
- Just hashtags with no substance

## Frequency & Timing

### How Often to Post?

**Daily**: Only if you have substantial updates
- Risk: Running out of things to say
- Benefit: High visibility

**2-3x per week**: Recommended for most
- Risk: Minimal
- Benefit: Consistent presence without burnout

**Weekly**: Minimum viable frequency
- Risk: Losing momentum/visibility
- Benefit: Each post is high-quality

**Ad-hoc/as things happen**: Not recommended
- Risk: Inconsistent, easily forgotten
- Benefit: All posts are significant

### Best Times to Post

**Twitter/X:**
- Weekdays 9am-11am (early morning scroll)
- Weekdays 1pm-3pm (lunch break)
- Evenings 7pm-9pm (after work)

**LinkedIn:**
- Tuesday-Thursday 8am-10am
- Lunch times 12pm-1pm
- Early evening 5pm-6pm

**General rule**: Post when YOUR audience is active. Test and measure.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### 1. Only Sharing Wins
**Problem**: Comes across as fake, braggy
**Solution**: Share struggles and setbacks too. Authenticity builds trust.

### 2. Sharing Everything
**Problem**: Info overload, noise, privacy concerns
**Solution**: Be selective. Not every commit needs a post. Focus on learnings.

### 3. No Clear Value
**Problem**: "Day 47 of building" with no substance
**Solution**: Every post should teach, inspire, or entertain. Give value.

### 4. Ignoring Engagement
**Problem**: Post and ghost - doesn't build community
**Solution**: Reply to comments, answer questions, engage back.

### 5. Being Inconsistent
**Problem**: Post 10 times one week, disappear for a month
**Solution**: Set a sustainable cadence and stick to it.

### 6. Too Much Hype
**Problem**: "GAME CHANGING! REVOLUTIONARY!" loses credibility
**Solution**: Let your work speak. Be excited but don't oversell.

### 7. No Story Arc
**Problem**: Random updates with no context
**Solution**: Help followers understand the journey. Reference previous posts.

### 8. Oversharing Sensitive Info
**Problem**: Customer data, internal conflicts, proprietary tech
**Solution**: Have a mental filter. When in doubt, don't share.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Be Authentic**: Fake positivity is obvious. Real struggles are relatable.

2. **Provide Value**: Every post should teach, inspire, or entertain.

3. **Stay Consistent**: Regular updates > occasional huge announcements.

4. **Engage**: Build in Public is about community, not broadcasting.

5. **Find Your Balance**: Not everything needs to be public. Protect what matters.

6. **Tell Stories**: People remember narratives, not feature lists.

7. **Show Your Work**: Process is often more interesting than final product.

8. **Be Patient**: Trust takes time to build. Play the long game.

## Quick Reference: Post Checklist

Before posting, ask yourself:

- [ ] Does this provide value to my audience?
- [ ] Am I being authentic and honest?
- [ ] Is there a clear hook/point?
- [ ] Does it tell a story or teach something?
- [ ] Have I included specific details/numbers?
- [ ] Is there a call-to-action or engagement hook?
- [ ] Is the tone conversational and natural?
- [ ] Have I proofread for typos?
- [ ] Am I comfortable sharing this publicly?
- [ ] Will Future Me be proud of this post?

If you answer "yes" to most of these, hit send!

**Remember**: Build in Public is about sharing your journey in a way that helps others on theirs. When in doubt, lead with empathy and value.
